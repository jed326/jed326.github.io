Reflection

Looking Back on English 202C

English 202C, my technical communication course at Penn State, has been an invaluable part of helping me improve my communication skills that will not only help me throughout the rest of my college education, but also throughout the rest of my professional career. This reflection revisits my takeaways from the course including some of the skills I have learned, some of the works, I have created, and how the course fits into my career as a whole.

Although I had always known technical communication was an essential part of being a valuable team member, I never really understood the mechanics behind good technical communication until my English 202C class at Penn State. The course introduced me to different good (and bad) technical writing practices, ranging from organization to document styling and helped me better understand how to communicate technical concepts effectively. As an aspiring software developer, the instruction set project, in particular, will help me better craft code documentation in the future.

In my e-portfolio, I have included a few writing samples that I created in my English 202C class. Specifically, I have included my technical writing analysis, my instruction set, and my technical definition and description. These writing assignments helped me hone my technical writing skills throughout the course, especially helping me improve my ability to clearly and logically organize my documents. I believe my writing samples here demonstrate that I am not only capable of creating clear, detailed technical documentation, but I am also capable of analyzing technical documentation created by other people. I hope this shows to employers that I will have no problem communicating with my future coworkers regardless of if I am communicating with coworkers with technical expertise or individuals who don’t have as much technical depth.

Although my computer science courses at Penn State have already helped me land summer internships at prestigious financial institutions such as J.P.Morgan Chase & Co. and Capital One, I know that career development and advancement relies much more on communication skills rather than purely technical skills. I believe that by improving my technical communication skills in English 202C this semester I am building valuable skills that will be important to call upon when presenting to my peers or supervisors.

I also believe that English 202C is an integral part of the puzzle that is my undergraduate education. First, as a Schreyer scholar, I am required to take add honors options to several of my courses, many of which include final paper or presentation projects as the centerpiece. Improving my technical writing skills will be an important part in improving my chances at success in these honors options. Additionally, as a Schreyer scholar I am also required to submit an honors thesis for graduation. Given that I plan on writing my thesis on the topic of cybersecurity, it will be incredibly important for me to communicate this technical subject to the Schreyer faculty and staff who may not have much technical knowledge about the field. Finally, I also perform research on campus at the Applied Research Laboratory, which includes biweekly technical presentations to my supervisor and division head. Even throughout this semester as I have been taking the class, I have seen the quality of my presentations improve as I implement new technical communication fundamentals such as clear consistent formatting throughout my presentation and using advance organizers to signpost what’s to come.

When I was designing my e-portfolio, I wanted to create something that would last beyond my English 202C class. I have always wanted to create my own website and start a blog on it, so this was the perfect opportunity for me to start. Because of this, I decided to not use any of the templates or options in Penn State’s sites system using WordPress, and instead create my e-portfolio using web development skills I have learned in school. Although my e-portfolio is focused on me, thanks to my English 202C class I also understand that the website must be easy for a user to navigate and use. Because of this, I chose to create a website that requires as few clicks as possible, since the user experience of loading new pages widely varies depending upon a user’s computer hardware and network connection. Instead, I chose a flowing single page format for the majority of my website, since the scrolling experience is essentially the same across all web browsing devices.

Thank you for visiting my site, if you have any feedback or would like to get to know me better, please reach out through the contact form!
