My Story

I was born in the small town of State College, Pennsylvania on January 4, 1998. My parents had immigrated to the United States from China just a few years earlier, making me the first American citizen in our family. My parents, cognizant of the opportunities they didn’t have in their own childhoods, supported me to explore as many activities and hobbies I could fill my time with.

Throughout my childhood and into high school, I played the piano and the saxophone, I participated in high school speech and debate, and was the varsity captain of the State College Area High School boys tennis team. These activities taught me the value of simply trying new things. In fact, that’s how I got drawn into computer science, the career path I’m currently on. In high school, I was looking for a math elective to take with one of my high school friends and we stumbled across the A Computer Science class at my school. The course showed me that computer science was essentially a whole new paradigm of problem solving and I was hooked immediately.

Fast forward a few years and now I am three semesters away from graduating with a degree in Computer Science from Penn State. At PSU I’ve taken courses that have challenged me to quickly learn new things, I have done undergraduate research at a research lab funded by the United States Navy, and I have even completed a summer internship at J.P.Morgan Chase & Co. For next summer, I have an internship lined up with Capital One and I can’t wait for what the future has to bring.
